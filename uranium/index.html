☢ Bull Thesis — Structural Deficit Through 2030s

The uranium market faces a persistent structural supply deficit. Primary production covers only ~72% of reactor demand. Secondary supply sources (stockpiles, recycling, underfeeding) are depleting. New mine development takes 10-15 years from discovery to production.

  • BofA, Goldman, and WNA all confirm structural deficit through 2030s
  • 62 reactors under construction globally (China, India leading) with 110+ planned
  • US Strategic Reserve purchases authorized ($2.72B over 10 years)
  • Kazatomprom (43% global supply) faces production challenges — sulfuric acid shortages
  • Japan reactor restarts adding 400-500 tonnes/year demand per restart
  • AI datacenter power demand accelerating nuclear renaissance narrative
